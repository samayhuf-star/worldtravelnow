Kenya Safari Guide: How to Plan the Trip of a Lifetime

📅 February 22, 2026⏰ 8 min read✍️ WorldTravel Now Editorial

A Kenya safari is one of those travel experiences that exists in a category of its own. Nothing quite prepares you for sitting in an open Land Cruiser as a lion pads across the grass twenty feet away, or watching a thousand wildebeest thunder through a river crossing during the Great Migration. If Africa has been on your list, Kenya is where you start.

The Maasai Mara: Africa's Crown Jewel

The Maasai Mara National Reserve is widely considered the finest wildlife destination in Africa. Year-round, it offers extraordinary big cat sightings — lions, leopards, and cheetahs are reliably spotted. The Great Wildebeest Migration between July and October — when over 1.5 million wildebeest, zebra, and gazelle cross from the Serengeti — is the largest overland animal migration in the world. The river crossings, where crocodiles lurk, are nature's most dramatic spectacle. Time your visit between August and September for the best chances.

Other Key Parks

Amboseli National Park, set against the backdrop of Mount Kilimanjaro, is famous for large elephant herds and iconic photography. Samburu National Reserve in the north offers unique species found nowhere else in Kenya — the reticulated giraffe, Grevy's zebra, and the long-necked gerenuk. Lake Nakuru can turn pink with flamingoes — one of the most surreal sights in nature.

Types of Safari

Budget safaris using minibuses start around $150–$200 per person per day including park fees. Mid-range tented camps offer a more immersive experience. Luxury lodge safaris ($500–$1,500+ per night) provide private game drives and expert naturalist guides. For a unique experience, consider a hot air balloon safari over the Mara at sunrise — around $450 per person, but unforgettable.

Nairobi: More Than a Gateway

The David Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage is a genuinely moving experience. The Giraffe Centre lets you feed Rothschild giraffes from an elevated platform. A meal at Carnivore Restaurant — Kenya's most famous dining institution — is a carnivore's dream.

Practical Tips

Get a yellow fever vaccination before travel. Pack neutral-colored clothing for game drives. Mornings (6–9am) and late afternoons (4–7pm) offer the best game viewing. Always follow your guide's instructions around wildlife. A Kenya safari will rearrange your priorities. After watching the sun set over the Mara with lions silhouetted in the foreground, everything else feels a little smaller.
